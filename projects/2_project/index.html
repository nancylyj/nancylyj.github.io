Multimodal Physiological Sensing

Multi-task learning for multiple physiological signals

Please refer to the paper for technical details.

As the COVID-19 pandemic has demonstrated a need for remote health care, non-contact physiological sensing with ubiquitous hardware such as cameras may enable medical practitioners to give educated medical advice in a remote setting. In this project, we seek to build comprehensive and robust model for multimodal signals including blood pressure, and facial action units for emotion detection.

Multi-task learning for multiple and highly diverse physiological signals

First, to make remote photoplethysmogram (rPPG) research more accessible and standardized, I contributed to an open-source toolbox that supports multiple public datasets, preprocessing methods, model training, and evaluation to help benchmark the performance of methods for camera physiological measurement.

Beyond this, I have been working on large multi-task training for highly diverse physiological modalities including blood pressure and facial action units for emotion detection built on top of the toolbox infrastructure. I have explored multiple public rPPG datasets, including PURE, UBFC, SCAMPS, and BP4D and preprocessed the large-scale video data and labels to make them compatible with the toolbox.
